Zebras :

Zebras are fascinating animals known for their distinctive black and white stripes. Here are some key facts about zebras:

Species

Zebras belong to the equine family and are native to Africa. There are three main species of zebras:

  • 1. Plains Zebra (Equus quagga)
  • 2. Grevy's Zebra (Equus grevyi)
  • 3. Mountain Zebra (Equus zebra)

Image of a Zebra :

Physical Characteristics

Zebras are known for their striking black and white stripes, which are unique to each individual. They have a horse-like body shape and stand about 4 to 5 feet tall at the shoulder. Zebras also have manes that stand upright.

Habitat

Zebras inhabit various regions of Africa, from grasslands to savannas. They are herbivores and primarily graze on grass.

Behavior

Zebras are social animals that often live in groups called "harems." They use their stripes for camouflage and to confuse predators. When threatened, zebras may run in a zigzag pattern to evade predators.

Conservation Status

While zebras are not currently considered endangered, some species, such as the Grevy's zebra, are listed as vulnerable due to habitat loss and hunting.

Fun Fact

Did you know that a group of zebras is called a "dazzle"?

These magnificent creatures continue to captivate people around the world with their unique appearance and behavior.
